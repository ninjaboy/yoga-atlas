# 📖 Foundational Texts

The source literature, oldest to newest. Many are freely readable on **Wikisource**,
**Archive.org** and **sacred-texts.com** (links below are open where they exist).

## Pre-classical & philosophical

| Text | Date | Attribution | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vedas** | c. 1500–500 BCE | anonymous, oral | ritual + hymns; the seedbed of ascetic/meditative ideas |
| **Upaniṣads** (esp. *Kaṭha*, *Śvetāśvatara*) | c. 800–200 BCE | various | first philosophical use of *yoga*; self (*ātman*), breath, meditation |
| **Bhagavad Gītā** | c. 200 BCE | part of the *Mahābhārata* | the three paths — karma, bhakti, jñāna ([[Paths-and-Lineages]]) |

## Classical yoga

### Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali
- **Date:** early centuries CE (estimates 2nd c. BCE – 4th c. CE; **unverifiable**).
- **Author:** the sage **Patañjali**, who **compiled** rather than invented — drawing on
  **Sāṃkhya, Buddhism and older yoga**.
- **Contents:** 196 terse aphorisms in 4 *pādas*; defines yoga as
  *citta-vṛtti-nirodha* (stilling the mind's fluctuations) and lays out the **eight limbs**
  (see [[Philosophy-and-Concepts]]). Āsana here = a *steady, comfortable seat*, nothing more.
- **Key commentaries:** Vyāsa's *Bhāṣya*; later Bhoja, Vācaspati Miśra.
- Read: [Yoga Sūtras — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoga_Sutras_of_Patanjali) ·
  open translations on [sacred-texts.com](https://sacred-texts.com/hin/index.htm).

## The Haṭha corpus (medieval)

| Text | Date | Author | Contents |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā (HYP)** | **15th c.** | **Svātmārāma** | the classic manual: āsana, prāṇāyāma, bandhas, mudrās, ṣaṭkarmas, nāda; a compilation of earlier haṭha texts |
| **Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā** | **c. 17th c.** | sage **Gheraṇḍa** | an "encyclopedia" of haṭha; the *saptāṅga* (seven-limb) scheme, 32 āsanas, kriyās |
| **Śiva Saṃhitā** | **c. 1300–1500** (Mallinson) | unknown | tantric-flavoured; subtle body, mudrās, the four kinds of yogin |
| **Gorakṣa Śataka** | c. 12th–13th c. | attrib. **Gorakṣanāth** | early Nāth haṭha; foundational to the subtle-body model |

> The HYP, Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā and Śiva Saṃhitā are the **three most influential surviving
> haṭha texts**; they're bundled in translations such as *The Forceful Yoga*
> (Bhatt/Sinh). ([Shiva Samhita — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiva_Samhita) · [HYP — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatha_Yoga_Pradipika))

## Visual / late-medieval source texts

- **Bahr al-ḥayāt** ("Ocean of Life", 16th c.) — earliest known **illustrated** āsana
  manuscript, commissioned for the Mughal emperor Jahāngīr.
- **Joga Pradīpikā** (18th c., attrib. Rāmānandi Jayatarāma) — illustrated āsanas + mudrās.
- **Sritattvanidhi** (19th c., Mysore) — **122 illustrated postures**, the most elaborate
  pre-20th-c. visual āsana text; a direct influence on Krishnamacharya's Mysore school.
  → images catalogued in [[Open-Media]].

## Influential modern books
- **Vivekānanda — *Rāja Yoga*** (1896): the text that framed yoga for the modern West.
- **B.K.S. Iyengar — *Light on Yoga*** (1966): the modern posture canon.
- **Mallinson & Singleton — *Roots of Yoga*** (2017): the scholarly source-anthology.
