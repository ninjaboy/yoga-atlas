🌬️ Practices — Breath, Locks, Cleansing & the Subtle Body

The non-āsana core of Haṭha yoga: working prāṇa (vital energy) through breath, locks, gestures and cleansing, toward meditation. Mechanics rest on the subtle-body model (nāḍīs, chakras, kuṇḍalinī).

Prāṇāyāma (breath regulation)

The 4th limb. Breath as the handle on prāṇa; classic ratio inhale:retain:exhale = 1:4:2. (Pranayama — Wikipedia)

Technique "…breath" What it does
Nāḍī Śodhana alternate-nostril balances the two hemispheres / nāḍīs; calming
Ujjāyī victorious partial glottis closure; warms, steadies (the "ocean" sound of flow yoga)
Kapālabhāti skull-shining forceful abdominal exhalations; energising (also a kriyā)
Bhastrikā bellows forceful in- and exhalation; heating
Śītalī / Sītkārī cooling tongue/teeth inhalation; cools
Bhrāmarī bee humming exhale; soothes the nervous system

⚠️ Retention (kumbhaka) and forceful breathing can over-stress; traditionally taught under a teacher, with care in pregnancy, hypertension, and cardiac conditions.

On prāṇāyāma, the Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā ties breath directly to the mind (HYP 2.2, trans. Pancham Sinh):

"Respiration being disturbed, the mind becomes disturbed. By restraining respiration, the Yogi gets steadiness of mind." — HYP 2.2 (Pancham Sinh, sacred-texts.com)

Bandhas (energy locks)

Muscular "locks" that contain and redirect prāṇa: - Mūla Bandha — root lock (pelvic floor) - Uḍḍiyāna Bandha — abdominal lift - Jālandhara Bandha — throat/chin lock - (All three together = Mahā Bandha.)

Mudrās (seals/gestures)

Direct prāṇa via gesture or whole-body seal: Yoga Mudrā, Viparīta Karaṇī, Khecarī, Yoni, Prāṇa mudrā. In Haṭha texts mudrās are powerful internal techniques, not just hand positions.

The Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā counts mudrā among its seven limbs and lists twenty-five of them (GhS 3.1–3, trans. Srisa Chandra Vasu):

"There are twenty-five mudrās, the practice of which gives success to the Yogis. They are: (1) Mahā-mudrā, (2) Nabho-mudrā, (3) Uḍḍiyāna, (4) Jālandhara, (5) Mūlabandha, (6) Mahābandha, (7) Mahāvedha, (8) Khechari, (9) Viparīta-kari, (10) Yoni…" — Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 3.1–3 (Srisa Chandra Vasu, archive.org)

Ṣaṭkarmas / Kriyās (six cleansings)

Purification before deeper practice (Hatha Yoga Pradipika): Neti (nasal), Dhauti (digestive tract), Nauli (abdominal churning), Basti (colon), Kapālabhāti (frontal cleansing), Trāṭaka (steady gazing).

Both classical manuals open the practical path with the ṣaṭkarmas. The Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā prescribes them for those of excess (HYP 2.21–22, trans. Pancham Sinh):

"If there be excess of fat or phlegm in the body, the six kinds of kriyas (duties) should be performed first. But others, not suffering from the excess of these, should not perform them." "The six kinds of duties are: Dhauti, Basti, Neti, Trataka, Nauti [Nauli] and Kapala Bhati. These are called the six actions." — HYP 2.21–22 (Pancham Sinh, sacred-texts.com)

The Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā likewise opens its seven-limbed ghaṭastha yoga with purification (GhS 1.12, trans. Srisa Chandra Vasu):

"(1) Dhauti; (2) Basti; (3) Neti; (4) Laukiki [Nauli]; (5) Trataka; (6) Kapalabhati are the Shatkarmas or six practices, known as Sadhana." — Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 1.12 (Srisa Chandra Vasu, archive.org)

Meditation & pratyāhāra

Limbs 5–8 of the eight-limbed path: pratyāhāra (sense withdrawal) → dhāraṇā (concentration) → dhyāna (meditation) → samādhi (absorption). The triad dhāraṇā-dhyāna-samādhi applied to one object = saṃyama.

The subtle body (the model these act on)

  • Prāṇa — vital energy, in five vāyus (prāṇa, apāna, samāna, udāna, vyāna).
  • Nāḍīs — channels; the three chief ones are iḍā, piṅgalā, suṣumnā.
  • Cakras — energy centres along suṣumnā (root → crown).
  • Kuṇḍalinī — latent energy "coiled" at the base, raised through suṣumnā by haṭha practice toward the crown (union).

⚠️ Classical vs modern: the subtle body is a traditional experiential/symbolic map, not a claim of physical anatomy. Modern wellness sometimes presents chakras as literal — the texts treat them as yogic, not biomedical.
