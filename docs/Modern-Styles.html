🌍 Modern Styles & Global Spread

How yoga became a worldwide, largely posture- and wellness-centred practice — and the branded styles that resulted. Lineage roots are in Paths and Lineages; the historical argument in History and Origins.

The spread (1893 → today)

  • 1893 — Vivekānanda at the Parliament of Religions opens the West to yoga (as philosophy/Rāja).
  • 1920s–40s — Yogendra, Kuvalayānanda and Krishnamacharya systematise posture practice in India, blending Haṭha with physical culture.
  • 1947–60s — Krishnamacharya's students (Iyengar, Jois, Devi, Desikachar) and Yogananda/Sivananda carry it abroad; Light on Yoga (1966) becomes the canon.
  • 1990s–2010s — studio boom, branded styles, the wellness/fitness turn, and athleisure; yoga becomes a mass-market activity.

The major branded styles

Style Founder / root Defining method
Iyengar B.K.S. Iyengar precise alignment, long holds, props
Ashtanga Vinyasa K. Pattabhi Jois fixed series, breath-linked (vinyāsa), dṛṣṭi
Vinyasa / Flow from Ashtanga breath-synchronised flow, sequences vary
Power Yoga Ashtanga-derived (Birch, Freeman) vigorous, fitness-focused flow, less ritual
Bikram Bikram Choudhury fixed 26 postures + 2 breaths in a ~40 °C room
Hot Yoga generic any yoga in a heated room; no fixed sequence (unlike Bikram)
Yin Paulie Zink / Paul Grilley, Sarah Powers long-held passive floor poses; connective tissue; Taoist influence
Restorative Iyengar-derived fully prop-supported rest; nervous-system recovery
Sivananda / Integral Sivananda lineage classical Haṭha + Rāja, the "Five Points"
Kuṇḍalinī Yogi Bhajan kriyās, mantra, breath to raise kuṇḍalinī

Bikram vs "hot": Bikram = one trademarked 26-pose sequence at a fixed temp; "hot yoga" is the genericised, format-free heated practice that largely replaced the Bikram brand after its founder's downfall. (Alive Studios)

Debates

  • Cultural appropriation / authenticity — tension between yoga as a Hindu/Indian spiritual tradition and its Western commodification (the "Namaste-on-a-tank-top" critique); countered by yoga's own long history of adaptation. Handle as a live, two-sided debate.
  • Teacher misconduct — several founders (Jois, Bikram Choudhury, Yogi Bhajan) have documented abuse records; relevant to how lineages are taught today.

Science & scale

  • Research: moderate evidence for benefits in flexibility, balance, stress, low-back pain and some mental-health measures; strong therapeutic claims are often over-stated — read trials critically.
  • Industry scale: the global yoga market was estimated at ~US$127 billion in 2025, projected toward ~US$269 billion by 2033 (~9.9% CAGR). Practitioners skew female (~72%) and age 30–50; Asia-Pacific ~37% of the market; most practice still in-person (~74%). (Grand View Research · Statista)

⚠️ Market figures are vendor market-research estimates and vary widely between firms — treat magnitudes, not precise numbers, as reliable.
