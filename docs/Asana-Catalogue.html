🤸 Asana Catalogue

Āsana (Sanskrit, "seat/posture"). In the Yoga Sūtras it means a steady, comfortable seat for meditation; the large posture repertoire is mostly medieval Haṭha and modern. The traditional symbolic count is 84, but texts list different selections; the Sritattvanidhi (19th c.) already illustrates 122.

Provenance flag (per Mallinson & Singleton, Roots of Yoga): seated/meditative and some inverted postures are genuinely old; most standing postures (e.g. the Warrior series, many balances) are early-20th-century developments from the Mysore school + physical culture. Marked ⟲ modern below.

Benefits & cautions (as commonly taught)

  • Standing → leg/core strength, balance. Backbends → spinal mobility, chest opening. Inversions → circulation, focus (caution: neck/eyes, blood pressure, pregnancy). Restorative → nervous-system down-regulation.
  • General caution: posture claims are traditional/experiential; for medical effects see the research summary in Modern Styles.
