👤 Key Figures

From legendary compilers to the modern teachers who built today's global practice.

Classical / legendary

  • Patañjali — compiler of the Yoga Sūtras (early centuries CE); systematised the eight-limbed Rāja yoga. Traditionally depicted as part-serpent (an incarnation of Ādiśeṣa). Iconography in Open Media.
  • Matsyendranāth & Gorakṣanāth (c. 10th–12th c.) — founders of the Nāth tradition; central to the medieval haṭha and subtle-body teachings.

The modern hinge

Figure Dates Contribution
Swami Vivekānanda 1863–1902 Introduced yoga to the West at the 1893 Parliament of Religions; Rāja Yoga (1896). The founder of "modern yoga" as a transnational idea.
Paramahansa Yogananda 1893–1952 Autobiography of a Yogi (1946); Self-Realization Fellowship (California); spread kriyā yoga.
Swami Sivananda 1887–1963 Physician → renunciate; Divine Life Society (Rishikesh, 1936); the "Five Points" → Paths and Lineages.

Krishnamacharya & his students — the source of modern posture yoga

  • T. Krishnamacharya (1888–1989) — the "father of modern yoga." Taught at the Mysore Palace, drawing on haṭha texts (incl. the Sritattvanidhi), Indian wrestling/ gymnastics and his own innovation. His students seeded most modern schools.
  • B.K.S. Iyengar (1918–2014) — Iyengar Yoga (alignment, props); Light on Yoga (1966); arguably the single greatest popularizer worldwide.
  • K. Pattabhi Jois (1915–2009) — Ashtanga Vinyasa; the breath-linked set series behind modern flow yoga. (Note: posthumous accounts of physical-adjustment abuse are part of his record and are documented factually in the wider literature.)
  • T.K.V. Desikachar (1938–2016) — Krishnamacharya's son; Viniyoga, individualised / therapeutic yoga (Krishnamacharya Yoga Mandiram).
  • Indra Devi (1899–2002) — one of Krishnamacharya's first female and Western students; early global popularizer ("First Lady of Yoga").

Other notable moderns

  • Swami Satyananda (1923–2009) — Bihar School of Yoga; systematised Yoga Nidrā.
  • Yogi Bhajan (1929–2004) — brought Kuṇḍalinī Yoga West (1969). (His legacy is contested; abuse allegations are documented — recorded here factually.)

Contemporary scholars

  • Mark Singleton & James Mallinson — historians who reframed how we understand yoga's past: Yoga Body (Singleton, 2010) and Roots of Yoga (2017). See History and Origins.
