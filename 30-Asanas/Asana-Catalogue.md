# 🤸 Asana Catalogue

*Āsana* (Sanskrit, "seat/posture"). In the [[Foundational-Texts|Yoga Sūtras]] it means a
*steady, comfortable seat* for meditation; the large posture repertoire is mostly
**medieval Haṭha and modern**. The traditional symbolic count is **84**, but texts list
different selections; the [[Open-Media|Sritattvanidhi]] (19th c.) already illustrates **122**.

> **Provenance flag** (per Mallinson & Singleton, *Roots of Yoga*): seated/meditative and
> some inverted postures are genuinely old; **most standing postures** (e.g. the Warrior
> series, many balances) are **early-20th-century** developments from the
> [[History-and-Origins|Mysore school + physical culture]]. Marked **⟲ modern** below.

## Standing (mostly ⟲ modern)
| Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tāḍāsana** | Mountain | the standing base/"neutral" |
| **Vīrabhadrāsana I–III** ⟲ | Warrior I–III | strength, balance; signature modern standing series |
| **Trikoṇāsana** ⟲ | Triangle | lateral stretch; legs/core |
| **Vṛkṣāsana** | Tree | balance |
| **Utkaṭāsana** | Chair | leg strength |

## Benefits & cautions (as commonly taught)
- Standing → leg/core strength, balance. Backbends → spinal mobility, chest opening.
  Inversions → circulation, focus (**caution**: neck/eyes, blood pressure, pregnancy).
  Restorative → nervous-system down-regulation.
- General caution: posture claims are **traditional/experiential**; for medical effects
  see the research summary in [[Modern-Styles]].
