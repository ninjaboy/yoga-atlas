# 🤸 Asana Catalogue

*Āsana* (Sanskrit, "seat/posture"). In the [[Foundational-Texts|Yoga Sūtras]] (Patañjali, 2.46)
it means only *sthira-sukham āsanam* — a **steady, comfortable seat** for meditation, with no
named postures at all. The large posture repertoire is mostly **medieval Haṭha and modern**.

The traditional symbolic count is **84** — derived from the legend (in the 13th-c.
*Dattātreyayogaśāstra* and the *Gorakṣaśataka*) that Śiva fashioned one āsana for each of the
8,400,000 species of living beings, then taught 84 to humankind. But the surviving texts each
list **different** selections, and most describe only a handful:

- The **Haṭhapradīpikā** (c. 15th c.) names 84 but describes only **15**, with benefits given for just 4.
- The **Haṭharatnāvalī** (17th c., Śrīnivāsa) is the **earliest text to actually list 84** names.
- The **Yogacintāmaṇi** (17th c.) and **Jogapradīpikā** (c. 1737) give fuller illustrated sets.
- The [[Open-Media|Śrītattvanidhi]] (19th-c. Mysore palace, attrib. Kṛṣṇarāja Wodeyar III) already
  illustrates **122** postures — direct ancestors of the Mysore school's modern repertoire.

> **Provenance flag** (per Mallinson & Singleton, *Roots of Yoga*, 2017). Until ~the 14th c. āsana
> served mainly as a **stable platform for prāṇāyāma, japa and meditation**, or to build ascetic
> *tapas* — so genuinely old postures are overwhelmingly **seated/meditative**, plus a few inversions,
> arm-balances and supine poses. **Most standing postures** (the Warrior series, the Triangle family,
> Downward Dog, the whole Sun-Salutation flow) are **early-20th-century** developments from the
> [[History-and-Origins|Mysore school + European physical culture]] (Krishnamacharya, Niels Bukh's
> 1924 *Primary Gymnastics*, Iyengar). These are marked **⟲ modern** below. *Do not assume a
> Sanskrit-sounding name means an ancient pose.*

## Standing (mostly ⟲ modern)

Almost the entire standing repertoire post-dates 1900. The medieval texts contain essentially
no standing balances; these were synthesised by Krishnamacharya (c. 1930s–40s) from gymnastics,
*sūrya-namaskāra* drills and wrestling culture, then spread by Iyengar and Pattabhi Jois.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tāḍāsana / Samasthiti** | Mountain | The standing "neutral" base; a 19th–20th-c. baseline pose. |
| **Vīrabhadrāsana I–III** ⟲ | Warrior I–III | Krishnamacharya, c. 1940, popularised by Iyengar; the signature modern series. Strength + balance. |
| **Utthita Trikoṇāsana** ⟲ | Extended Triangle | Lateral stretch for legs/spine; first appears in the 20th c. |
| **Parivṛtta Trikoṇāsana** ⟲ | Revolved Triangle | Twisting variant; 20th-c. (Mysore). |
| **Utthita Pārśvakoṇāsana** ⟲ | Extended Side Angle | 20th-c.; deep side-body and hip stretch. |
| **Vṛkṣāsana** | Tree | Standing balance. A tree-named tapas posture is *old* (one-legged ascetic standing), but the modern foot-to-thigh form is a 20th-c. restyling. |
| **Utkaṭāsana** ⟲ | Chair / Fierce | Leg strength; the modern seated-in-air form is 20th-c. (the older *utkaṭāsana* was a squat). |
| **Garuḍāsana** ⟲ | Eagle | Wrapped-limb balance; modern. |
| **Naṭarājāsana** ⟲ | Lord of the Dance | Standing backbend balance; 20th-c. |
| **Adho Mukha Śvānāsana** ⟲ | Downward-Facing Dog | Core of the modern Sun Salutation; 20th-c. (Krishnamacharya). |
| **Añjaneyāsana** ⟲ | Low Lunge / Crescent | 20th-c. lunge family. |

## Seated & forward bends (the old core)

This is where the genuinely ancient material lives: the meditation seats and a few seated
forward folds are documented from the medieval Haṭha corpus (and some from earlier Upaniṣads).

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sukhāsana** | Easy seat | Basic cross-legged meditation seat. |
| **Padmāsana** | Lotus | Classic meditative āsana; described in the Haṭhapradīpikā and far older. |
| **Siddhāsana** | Accomplished pose | The core Haṭha seat; one of only two poses named in the *Gorakṣaśataka*. |
| **Svastikāsana** | Auspicious / good-luck seat | One of the HYP's 15 named āsanas. |
| **Bhadrāsana / Gorakṣāsana** | Gentle / Goraksha's pose | HYP's 15; named for the Nāth founder [[Key-Figures|Gorakṣanāth]]. |
| **Vajrāsana** | Thunderbolt / kneeling | Traditional meditative/digestive kneel. |
| **Vīrāsana** | Hero | One of the HYP's 15 named āsanas. |
| **Daṇḍāsana** | Staff | Seated base for the forward-bend family. |
| **Paścimottānāsana** | Seated Forward Bend | One of only **4** poses the HYP gives benefits for; genuinely old. Spine + hamstrings. |
| **Jānuśīrṣāsana** ⟲ | Head-to-Knee | The modern asymmetric form is 20th-c. (Krishnamacharya). |
| **Gomukhāsana** | Cow-Faced | Documented from the *Darśana Upaniṣad* and HYP; very old. |
| **Kūrmāsana** | Tortoise | Appears in early texts (*Ahirbudhnya Saṃhitā*); HYP's 15. |
| **Baddha Koṇāsana** | Bound Angle / Cobbler | Hip-opener; older lineage (a "throne" seat). |
| **Upaviṣṭa Koṇāsana** ⟲ | Wide-Angle Seated Forward Bend | Modern. |

## Twists

A spinal twist (*Matsyendrāsana*) is genuinely medieval; the modern half/quarter variants and
the chair/Marichi twists are 20th-c. refinements.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Ardha Matsyendrāsana** | Half Lord of the Fishes | Named for the Nāth founder [[Key-Figures|Matsyendranāth]]. The full *Matsyendrāsana* is one of the HYP's 15. |
| **Marīcyāsana** (A–D) ⟲ | Marichi's Pose | Bound seated twists; 20th-c. (Mysore). |
| **Bharadvājāsana** ⟲ | Bharadvaja's Twist | Gentle seated twist; modern. |

## Backbends

Cobra, Bow and Locust have medieval roots; the deep standing/wheel/king-pigeon backbends are
later or 20th-c.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Bhujaṅgāsana** | Cobra | Gentle prone backbend; older lineage. Chest/spine. |
| **Śalabhāsana** | Locust | Prone backbend; the modern form is documented via Theos Bernard (20th-c.) though "locust" is older. |
| **Dhanurāsana** | Bow | One of the HYP's 15 named āsanas; full backbend. |
| **Uṣṭrāsana** | Camel | Kneeling heart-opener; later/modern systematisation. |
| **Matsyāsana** | Fish | In the *Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā* (17th c.); a counter-pose to the shoulderstand. |
| **Setu Bandha Sarvāṅgāsana** | Bridge | Appears as *Kāmapīṭhāsana* in the 19th-c. Śrītattvanidhi. |
| **Ūrdhva Dhanurāsana / Cakrāsana** ⟲ | Wheel / Upward Bow | Deep backbend; the modern full-wheel form is 20th-c. |
| **Eka Pāda Rājakapotāsana** ⟲ | One-Legged King Pigeon | 20th-c. hip-opening backbend. |

## Inversions (older lineage)

Inversions are among the *oldest* non-seated practices — Haṭha texts treat the inverted
**Viparīta Karaṇī** as a *mudrā* (a means to retain *amṛta*/bindu), not mere exercise.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Śīrṣāsana** | Headstand | "King of āsanas" (Iyengar). Inverted postures are attested medievally, though the precise tripod/forearm forms were standardised in the 20th c. |
| **Sarvāṅgāsana** | Shoulderstand | "Queen of āsanas"; modern systematisation of an older inverted practice. |
| **Halāsana** | Plough | Modern form, older inverted lineage. |
| **Viparīta Karaṇī** | Legs-up / Inverted Action | Classed as a **mudrā** in the Haṭha corpus; genuinely old. |

## Arm balances & core (mostly ⟲ modern, a few old)

Surprisingly, two arm-balances — Peacock and Cockerel — are genuinely *medieval*; the rest of
the crow/plank/boat family is 20th-c.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mayūrāsana** | Peacock | One of the HYP's 15; attested from c. 10th c. (*Vimānārcanākalpa*). Genuinely old. |
| **Kukkuṭāsana** | Cockerel | One of the HYP's 15; lotus-through-arms balance. Old. |
| **Bakāsana / Kākāsana** ⟲ | Crane / Crow | Modern arm balance. |
| **Caturaṅga Daṇḍāsana** ⟲ | Four-Limbed Staff | The "yoga push-up"; 20th-c. (Sun Salutation). |
| **Vasiṣṭhāsana** ⟲ | Side Plank | 20th-c. (Krishnamacharya). |
| **Nāvāsana** ⟲ | Boat | Core pose; modern. |

## Restorative / supine

The corpse pose is genuinely old (one of the HYP's 4 benefit-bearing poses); the rest is
modern restorative practice.

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Śavāsana** | Corpse | One of the HYP's named/benefit-giving āsanas; final relaxation, deceptively essential. |
| **Bālāsana** ⟲ | Child's Pose | Modern resting pose. |
| **Supta Baddha Koṇāsana** ⟲ | Reclined Bound Angle | Restorative hip-opener; modern. |
| **Supta Vajrāsana** | Reclined Thunderbolt | Reclined kneel; older lineage. |
| **Makarāsana** | Crocodile | Prone relaxation pose. |

## Benefits & cautions (as commonly taught)

| Family | Common benefits | Key cautions |
|---|---|---|
| **Standing** | Leg/core strength, balance, grounding. | Knee tracking; avoid locking joints; balance poses risky with vertigo/inner-ear issues. |
| **Seated / forward bends** | Hip & hamstring length, calming; the meditation seats steady the spine for prāṇāyāma. | Lumbar disc issues — hinge from the hips, not the waist; force nothing. |
| **Twists** | Spinal mobility, perceived digestive stimulation. | Pregnancy (closed twists), SI-joint and disc problems. |
| **Backbends** | Spinal extension, chest/heart opening, counter to desk posture. | Lower-back compression; recent abdominal surgery; cervical strain in deep variants. |
| **Inversions** | Circulation, lymphatic return, focus; traditionally "preserve the *amṛta*". | **Neck/eyes, high or low blood pressure, glaucoma/retinal issues, pregnancy, menstruation (debated), cervical injury.** |
| **Arm balances / core** | Upper-body & core strength, proprioception. | Wrist injury (carpal tunnel), shoulder instability. |
| **Restorative / supine** | Nervous-system down-regulation, recovery, sleep. | Mostly safe; support the lower back/knees with props. |

- General caution: posture benefit-claims are **traditional/experiential**, not medical prescriptions;
  for evidence-based effects see the research summary in [[Modern-Styles]].
