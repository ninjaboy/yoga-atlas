# 🌬️ Practices — Breath, Locks, Cleansing & the Subtle Body

The non-āsana core of Haṭha yoga: working **prāṇa** (vital energy) through breath, locks,
gestures and cleansing, toward meditation. Mechanics rest on the
[[Philosophy-and-Concepts|subtle-body]] model (nāḍīs, chakras, kuṇḍalinī).

## Prāṇāyāma (breath regulation)

The 4th limb. Breath as the handle on prāṇa; classic ratio inhale:retain:exhale = **1:4:2**.
([Pranayama — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pranayama))

| Technique | "…breath" | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Nāḍī Śodhana** | alternate-nostril | balances the two hemispheres / nāḍīs; calming |
| **Ujjāyī** | victorious | partial glottis closure; warms, steadies (the "ocean" sound of flow yoga) |
| **Kapālabhāti** | skull-shining | forceful abdominal **exhalations**; energising (also a kriyā) |
| **Bhastrikā** | bellows | forceful **in- and exhalation**; heating |
| **Śītalī / Sītkārī** | cooling | tongue/teeth inhalation; cools |
| **Bhrāmarī** | bee | humming exhale; soothes the nervous system |

> ⚠️ Retention (*kumbhaka*) and forceful breathing can over-stress; traditionally taught
> **under a teacher**, with care in pregnancy, hypertension, and cardiac conditions.

![Nāḍī-śodhana (alternate-nostril breathing): seated practitioner closing one nostril to balance the iḍā/piṅgalā nāḍīs.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ee/Respiraci%C3%B3n_alterna.jpg)
*Source: "Respiración alterna", Wikimedia Commons. Licence: CC BY-SA / GFDL. Attribution: Kcam.*

On prāṇāyāma, the **Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā** ties breath directly to the mind (HYP 2.2, trans. Pancham Sinh):

> "Respiration being disturbed, the mind becomes disturbed. By restraining respiration, the Yogi gets steadiness of mind."
> — *HYP 2.2 ([Pancham Sinh, sacred-texts.com](https://www.sacred-texts.com/hin/hyp/hyp04.htm))*

## Bandhas (energy locks)
Muscular "locks" that contain and redirect prāṇa:
- **Mūla Bandha** — root lock (pelvic floor)
- **Uḍḍiyāna Bandha** — abdominal lift
- **Jālandhara Bandha** — throat/chin lock
- *(All three together = **Mahā Bandha**.)*

## Mudrās (seals/gestures)
Direct prāṇa via gesture or whole-body seal: **Yoga Mudrā**, **Viparīta Karaṇī**,
**Khecarī**, **Yoni**, **Prāṇa** mudrā. In Haṭha texts mudrās are powerful internal
techniques, not just hand positions.

![Mahā Mudrā ("great seal") — a whole-body mudrā, from an illustrated manuscript of the Joga Pradīpikā, c. 1830.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/Mahamudra_in_Jogapradipika.jpg)

*Source: Joga Pradīpikā, c. 1830 manuscript, via Wikimedia Commons. Licence: Public Domain (pre-1900 work of art).*

The **Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā** counts mudrā among its seven limbs and lists twenty-five of them (GhS 3.1–3, trans. Srisa Chandra Vasu):

> "There are twenty-five mudrās, the practice of which gives success to the Yogis. They are: (1) Mahā-mudrā, (2) Nabho-mudrā, (3) Uḍḍiyāna, (4) Jālandhara, (5) Mūlabandha, (6) Mahābandha, (7) Mahāvedha, (8) Khechari, (9) Viparīta-kari, (10) Yoni…"
> — *Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 3.1–3 ([Srisa Chandra Vasu, archive.org](https://archive.org/details/Gheranda_Samhita))*

## Ṣaṭkarmas / Kriyās (six cleansings)
Purification before deeper practice ([Hatha Yoga Pradipika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatha_Yoga_Pradipika)):
**Neti** (nasal), **Dhauti** (digestive tract), **Nauli** (abdominal churning),
**Basti** (colon), **Kapālabhāti** (frontal cleansing), **Trāṭaka** (steady gazing).

![Nauli — abdominal isolation/churning, the recti drawn into a central column (here with uḍḍiyāna).](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/69/Nauli.jpg)
*Source: "Nauli", Wikimedia Commons. Licence: CC BY-SA 2.5. Attribution: Gilloualou.*

Both classical manuals open the practical path with the ṣaṭkarmas. The **Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā** prescribes them for those of excess (HYP 2.21–22, trans. Pancham Sinh):

> "If there be excess of fat or phlegm in the body, the six kinds of kriyas (duties) should be performed first. But others, not suffering from the excess of these, should not perform them."
> "The six kinds of duties are: Dhauti, Basti, Neti, Trataka, Nauti [Nauli] and Kapala Bhati. These are called the six actions."
> — *HYP 2.21–22 ([Pancham Sinh, sacred-texts.com](https://www.sacred-texts.com/hin/hyp/hyp04.htm))*

The **Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā** likewise opens its seven-limbed *ghaṭastha* yoga with purification (GhS 1.12, trans. Srisa Chandra Vasu):

> "(1) Dhauti; (2) Basti; (3) Neti; (4) Laukiki [Nauli]; (5) Trataka; (6) Kapalabhati are the Shatkarmas or six practices, known as Sadhana."
> — *Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 1.12 ([Srisa Chandra Vasu, archive.org](https://archive.org/details/Gheranda_Samhita))*

## Meditation & pratyāhāra
Limbs 5–8 of the [[Philosophy-and-Concepts|eight-limbed path]]: **pratyāhāra** (sense
withdrawal) → **dhāraṇā** (concentration) → **dhyāna** (meditation) → **samādhi**
(absorption). The triad dhāraṇā-dhyāna-samādhi applied to one object = **saṃyama**.

## The subtle body (the model these act on)
- **Prāṇa** — vital energy, in five *vāyus* (prāṇa, apāna, samāna, udāna, vyāna).
- **Nāḍīs** — channels; the three chief ones are **iḍā**, **piṅgalā**, **suṣumnā**.
- **Cakras** — energy centres along suṣumnā (root → crown).
- **Kuṇḍalinī** — latent energy "coiled" at the base, raised through suṣumnā by haṭha
  practice toward the crown (union).

> ⚠️ **Classical vs modern:** the subtle body is a **traditional experiential/symbolic**
> map, not a claim of physical anatomy. Modern wellness sometimes presents chakras as
> literal — the texts treat them as yogic, not biomedical.
